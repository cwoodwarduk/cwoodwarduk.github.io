About Me

It all started in my teenage years being "that guy" who coded my friends' MySpace pages. I did all this from my whirring, spluttering first laptop, which I inherited from my father; spending hours googling various fixes that took me to the command line time and time again. And then at university I took things a step further by studying web design as out-of-department credits. The itch to code has been around ever since.

These days I am a full-stack developer and Agile-certified Scrum Master, with an inclination towards backend technologies. As a graduate of Mayden Academy, I have a strong grounding in professional standards such as SOLID and DRY priniciples, as well as OOP, use of frameworks and design patterns, and use of Git and compilers in my workflow.

In my free time I enjoy cycling ridiculous distances for coffee and cake, cooking Asian food, eating too much, and reading. I also speak decent Korean, having spent 6 years in South Korea, and also make rice wine from time to time.
